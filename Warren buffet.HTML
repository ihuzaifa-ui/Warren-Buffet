Warren Buffett: The Oracle of Omaha

Warren Buffett, often called the "Oracle of Omaha," is one of the most successful and respected investors in history. Born on August 30, 1930, in Omaha, Nebraska, Buffett developed an early interest in business and investing. Today, he is the chairman and CEO of Berkshire Hathaway, a multinational conglomerate known for its long-term value investing approach.

Early Life and Career

Warren Buffett showed exceptional business instincts at a young age. From selling soft drinks to running a pinball machine business, he always explored opportunities for profit. He later studied under the legendary investor Benjamin Graham at Columbia University, who introduced him to the principles of value investing.

Investment Philosophy

Warren Buffett’s strategy revolves around buying undervalued companies with strong fundamentals and holding them for the long term. His famous principles include:

  • Invest in what you understand
  • Focus on intrinsic value
  • Be fearful when others are greedy, and greedy when others are fearful
  • Avoid unnecessary risks and debt

Berkshire Hathaway Under Buffett’s leadership, Berkshire Hathaway grew from a struggling textile company to a powerhouse owning major businesses like GEICO, Dairy Queen, and BNSF Railway, along with large investments in Apple, CocaCola, and American Express.

Legacy and Influence

Buffett is admired not only for his investing success but also for his humility, discipline, and commitment to philanthropy. Through the Giving Pledge, he has promised to donate most of his wealth to charitable causes.
